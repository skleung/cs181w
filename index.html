Internet Vigilantism

A study of Code and the regulation of the Internet.

Overview

Vigilantism presents an ambiguous moral space, especially when examined in the context of the web. Something that may be more clearly wrong in real life becomes less clear when it occurs online. When individuals and independent organizations take law enforcement into their own hands on the Internet, both deontological moral precedent and utilitarianism come into question. Here, we expand on these ethical perspectives into policy recommendations.
